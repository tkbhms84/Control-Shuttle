What Is A Control Panel ?

The Space Shuttle was a partially reusable low Earth orbital spacecraft system operated from 1981 to 2011 by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) as part of the Space Shuttle program.Parts of a Space Shuttle are :-

  • - Orbiter
  • - Main Engines
  • - External Tanks And Boosters
  • - Control Panel

It controls the shuttle

The Control Panel is the steering wheel of the spaceshuttle . It is the place where the astronauts control the shuttle.

It Is The Contact Room

The Control Panel is the sole place in the spaceship where the astronauts can contact with the people on the Planet Earth.

It Is The Safehouse

The Control Panel is one of the safest places in the spaceship. It is the place where people gather at times of emergency

It Is The Information-Provider

It provides exchange of information between the headquarters at NASA and the astronauts.

Into The Space!

The space shuttle was 184 feet long. The orbiter was 122 feet long. The gross liftoff weight of the space shuttle was 4.5 million pounds.

Five shuttles flew into space during the program's history: Atlantis, Challenger, Columbia, Discovery and Endeavour.

The space shuttle Atlantis is seen over the Bahamas prior to a perfect docking with the International Space Station at 11:07 a.m. (EDT) on July 10, 2011 on Flight Day 3 of NASA's last shuttle flight.

NASA retired the space shuttle to devote its resources to sending astronauts beyond low-Earth orbit, to an asteroid, back to the moon and eventually Mars.
